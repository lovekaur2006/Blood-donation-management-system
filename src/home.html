Learn About Donation

The average human body contains about five liters of blood, which is made of several cellular and non-cellular components such as red blood cells, platelets, and plasma. Each type of component has its unique properties and can be used for different indications. The donated blood is separated into these components by the blood center, and one donated unit can save up to four lives depending on the number of components separated from your blood.

Packed Red Blood Cells

What is it? Blood collected straight from the donor into a blood bag and mixed with an anticoagulant is called as whole blood. This collected whole blood is then centrifuged, and red cells, platelets, and plasma are separated.

Who can donate? You need to be 18-65 years old, weigh 45kg or more, and be fit and healthy.

Used For? Correction of severe anemia and blood loss in childbirth, surgery, or trauma settings.

Lasts For? Red cells can be stored for 42 days at 2-6 degree Celsius.

How often can I donate? Male donors can donate again after 90 days, and female donors can donate again after 120 days.

Plasma

What is it? Plasma is the liquid portion of blood that contains water, salts, enzymes, antibodies, and other proteins. It is separated from the blood and used for various treatments.

Who can donate? Plasma donors must meet the same criteria as blood donors and should not have had recent infections or health conditions.

Used For? Plasma is essential in treating clotting disorders, burns, and immune system deficiencies.

How long does it take to donate? Plasma donation takes about 45-60 minutes.

Platelets

What is it? Platelets are tiny cell fragments in the blood that help with clotting. They are collected via a special process called apheresis.

Who can donate? Healthy adults aged 18-60 can donate platelets, provided they meet donation criteria.

Used For? Platelets are used to treat cancer patients and those with clotting disorders or undergoing major surgeries.

How often can I donate? Platelet donors can donate every 7 days, up to 24 times a year.
